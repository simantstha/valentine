A Letter For You

My dearest,

Some things in life arrive quietly — a notification, a match, a conversation that starts small and slowly becomes everything. That was us. We found each other on Hinge, but I like to think the universe had already decided it long before we did.

I still think about the day we first went to Mall of America — the way you walked beside me, the way everything felt easy and unhurried, the way I quietly thought to myself: this is someone I want to keep around. There was no grand moment, no dramatic scene. Just us, together, and it already felt like home.

What I admire most about you is something you probably don't even think about — your caring heart. There was a time you weren't well, tired and hurting, and yet — you were asking if I had eaten. You were telling me to rest. In that moment I saw all of you: the quiet strength you carry, the love you give without even realising it, the way you pour into others even when you yourself need to be poured into. That kind of love is rare. You are rare.

You are my best friend, my confidant, my calm. You are the person I want to tell things to — the funny things, the hard things, the things I haven't found words for yet. You are my favourite part of ordinary days.

Like spring — your favourite season — you bring warmth after cold, colour after grey, and something that feels like a beginning every single time I'm with you. Even the ordinary light looks different when you're in it.

You mean the world to me. Not the version of that phrase people say easily — I mean it the way where the world is quieter without you in it, where I notice your absence in the smallest moments, where choosing you feels less like a decision and more like breathing.

Thank you for being you. Every soft, caring, quietly beautiful part of you.

Forever yours, 🌹
